The Process

Craftsmanship

Every piece is made by hand in my small coastal studio — from sketch to finish, no machines, no shortcuts.

01

Sketch & Design

Each piece begins as a pencil sketch in my journal, inspired by tidal patterns, geological formations, and botanical forms.

02

Forge & Form

Using recycled precious metals, I hand-forge each piece at my bench with hammer, torch, and traditional silversmithing techniques.

03

Set & Finish

Stones are hand-set, surfaces textured or polished, and patinas applied. Each piece is one-of-a-kind — slight variations are part of the beauty.

04

Package & Ship

Wrapped in linen and housed in handmade boxes from local paper artisans. Shipped plastic-free with a signed certificate of authenticity.

The Maker

About the Artist

Hi, I'm Maëlle. I'm a self-taught metalsmith working from a converted garage studio in Cannon Beach, Oregon. I've been making jewelry since 2015, when I traded a career in architecture for a torch and an anvil.

My work is rooted in the Oregon landscape — I find most of my stones beachcombing, and every texture in my pieces comes from hammering on a little steel block I found on the beach our first week here. The imperfections are what make each piece alive.

I use 100% recycled gold and silver, source stones ethically, and ship everything plastic-free. Sustainability isn't a brand value — it's just how I make things.
